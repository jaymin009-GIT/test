Implementing Sales and Operations Planning Process in an Organisation

By
Chaitanya Lad
-
December 19, 2018

Sales and Operations Planning (S&OP) is a process for integrated decision making in your business, involving Sales, Operations and Finance where all these departments come together to work on a long-term & critical reviewing mid-term plan for the business.

The typical standard S&OP process includes a latest updated forecast that leads to a sales plan, production plan, inventory plan, customer lead time (backlog) plan, new product development plan, strategic initiative plan and finally resulting in the financial plan. The Plan frequency and planning horizon can vary & it depends on the specifics of the industry. The planning horizon for a typical S&OP process is long-term and extends over 18–36 months & it supports the strategic planning of the organization.

An ideal implemented S&OP process regularly reviews the customer demand and supply resources and “re-plans” quantitatively across an agreed rolling horizon. The re-planning process focuses on changes from the previously agreed sales and operations plan, while it helps the management team to understand how the company achieved its current level of performance, its primary focus is on future actions and anticipated results.

Over the period Sales and operations planning has become the most critical business process adopted to manage the balance and trade-off between the conflicting preferences of the supply and demand side of the supply chain and offers many value creation opportunities within the organization.

5 Critical pre-requisite steps for a successful S&OP process are as below.

  1. Managing Demand (Forecasting)- Demand Review
  2. Managing Supply – Supply Review
  3. Managing New Initiatives (Products, Customer and Market initiatives)
  4. Demand-Supply reconciliation (Pre-S&OP meeting)
  5. Review by Senior Business Management -Executive S&OP meeting

5 Best practices for getting maximum output out of the existing or to be implemented S&OP process

The standard S&OP process seems a complex process to make the things simple & effective at a workplace. This requires proper implementation of the process by following that some practices are implemented in step by step manner.

  1. Link Key Processes in the organization

In every organization, there will be processes which are done in silos & may be getting done multiple times by different teams. There can be processes which are existing in the organization which is dependent on each other. Don’t discard your existing standalone processes such as sales forecasting or production planning. Instead, make sure there are appropriate links between existing processes and that your people understand each other’s needs. Similarly, don’t think all processes need to be managed by a single all-encompassing system. For instance, sales forecasting, production planning, and capacity planning can all be done on separate systems if they are linked by using one monthly validated and approved sales forecast.

  1. Commitment/Executive Ownership for the S&OP

S&OP is a major business process that manages the balance and trade-offs between the conflicting preferences of Sales and Operations(supply). It is therefore very important to have effective leadership. Best practice suggests it be owned by the CEO or General Manager. If not, then a strong coalition between your Sales and Operations Leaders is required. This coalition must be able to manage the tension between these functions and set clear ground rules and boundaries for working together.

  1. Carefully consider External Factors

Always make sure that you have reviewed and understood external influences before you focus on internal activities. For example, has your Sales forecast adequately assessed assumptions, risk and opportunities, competitors’ actions and customers’ acceptance of new products? Similarly, do you understand lead time for capital equipment to increase capacity? Has there been a dramatic increase in supply lead time for a critical production component? These factors play a very important role in any decision/action you will be taking in coming near future.

  1. Focus on Key Information

Your business should have a tolerance for some level of sales forecast and production level variability, say +/-20% or increasing in far-off months. Don’t focus on the steady state. Try to understand where you see vast forecast over or under consumption or major production constraints. Which customers and products are affected and why? How long will the change last? Does it justify additional capital investments or re-tooling of idle capacity? For your executive-led S&OP to be effective you must highlight just the specific information required for decision making. And more importantly, all the stakeholders should work on one set off numbers across the organization without fail.

  1. Continuously Review the Plan

Reviews of performance against sales and operations plans are needed to prompt re-planning when necessary. Overall, the company must consider the continuous improvement to be an important ingredient in further improving the S&OP process. A successful supply chain focused company views S&OP as a journey, not a destination which evolves over the period!

Major Benefits of an Effective S&OP process

  1. Better integration between different functions
  2. Bad news first, transparency in the system
  3. Whole organization works to one set of numbers
  4. All teams gets aligned to common goal for the organization
  5. Allows you to shape demand
  6. Eliminates the battle over ownership of finished goods inventory

To summarise, the S&OP process implementation in an organization can be made simpler & smoother with ownership from leaders in the organization like MD/CEO or leaders from both Sales & Supply side. Once it is aligned at a top level, it is about rolling it at lower levels after taking people in confidence. It can be a big shift in the way organization is currently working & will need a lot of change management & people buy-in. But yes, in the end, the fruits of a well implemented S&OP process are sweet & everybody would enjoy it in the organization which justifies the pain that people might go through in the transition period.
